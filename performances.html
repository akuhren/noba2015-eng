Ikaros

Ikaros - by Kaj Nissen


The play is a part of Kaj Nissens “The Dead of the Danube”. In “The Dead of the Danube” we meet (which might sound a tad bit quaint) the dead who are buried in the “Cemetery of the Nameless”, reserved mainly for the bodies who are washed up face-down on the beach. Those are inevitably non-descript, have neither a name nor a curriculum vitae but in the play they all get to have a destiny. “Ikaros” is one such destiny: A man hurling towards the earth in free fall, recently being evicted from a perfectly good airplane. Why he finds himself in this particular predicament is gradually revealed to him (as well as the audience) on his way down…

Here he has also been an actor in several plays.

Metamorphosis

"Metamorphosis" by Franz Kafka


Franz Kafka’s “Metamorphosis” deals with human dignity and relations. It deals with the consequences of work’s and society’s ever growing demands to man.

Gregor Samsa wakes up one morning and finds that he is becoming an insect. Imprisoned in his own psyche, he rapidly grows incapable of communicating with the world surrounding him, or even his family.

Human values and relations cease to have relevance. Family life ends up in prejudice, brutality, loneliness, and human dread and disgust of everything strange.

In connection with Damir “Dado” Pudar’s innovative physical scenography, “Metamor-phosis” becomes a gripping scenic tale. Duration:  50 minutes.

Double Murder at Rosvang

Kulturforeningen Rosvang, Danmark

(Rosvang v. Sjørring)

DATE: JULI  7.TH - 19.30

Double Murder at Rosvang by Knud Hove


This year’s performance is a whodunit – comedy: “Double Murder at Rosvang”.

The piece differs in several ways from earlier performances. Amongst others, we have moved the staging to a different area of the park and also, we have been given the opportunity to include the main buildings in our plot. For the first time the piece is not historically related to the manor.

On the other hand, we try to be faithful to the era portrayed, as costumes and props strife to be historically correct. The plot takes place in 1958 and contains exactly the elements we deemed necessary to stage a crime story at Rosvang. There are ‘probably’ several murders. Also, there is a very entrepreneurial undertaker and likewise photographer, a cook who makes Femø-porridge every Saturday and finally yet importantly, some police with a finely tuned nose for crime. We consider the performance a comedy… There are 60 participants on stage, 12 outfitters, 10 directors/assistants and 6 musicians.  

‘The Cultural Association of Rosvang’ is an amateur ensemble which resides at the manor Rosvang in Thy. The company has existed for 7 years and has had a performance every other year since then.  The performance is always taking place in Rosvang’s Grounds. There are between 50 and 60 participants on stage and 25 to 30 practical assistants. Typically, there are between 15 and 20 children amongst the performers.

The Long Christmas Dinner

"The Long Christas Dinner"  by Thornton Wilder

 

All over the world, in many places, also in Latvia, every year, families come together for Christmas: every year there is the same food on the table, the same melodies can be heard, and the same situations and conversations keep repeating from year to year...

The play is about a family, who meet for every Christmas during almost a century – the story tells a lot about their relations, hopes, losses and pain.

Roderick and his new wife Lucia celebrate their very first Christmas in their new home – they are having a delicious turkey roast, drinking a  wonderful  wine and listening to the stories of the old mother. About 5 years later cousin Brendon, who has returned from Alaska, joins the family. Mother Bayard dies, Roderick and Lucia are having kids – son Charles and a daughter Genevieve.. but time passes by, Roderick gets old and dies, his son Charles gets married to Leonora and experiences the death of his first child. Brendon and Lucia pass away, but the family has been enriched with their grandchildren – twins Sam and Lucia.. As time goes by, Sam dies during the war in Europe, but the casual Christmas dinner order is disrupted by the new generation, for whom  the family home has become too small and who leaves it for good. The home gets sad without young people, but they are making new homes somewhere in the world, gathering together for Christmas.. and the traditions of family continue to live.


Liepaja Theater of Liepaja Culture center has been operating since 1954. The group stages both global and Latvian classics as well as plays by contemporary authors. Since 1995 the leader and director of the theater is Inara Kalnaraja. Beside plays the group stages also performances based on folk songs, poetry and music. The theatre usually plays its performances in their home city Liepaja, but quite often the group is invited to perform also in other cities of Latvia. - The composition of the group is variable, but mostly there are about 20-30 active members of theatre, representing all the generations, because the age gratitude in the group is from 7–80 years. Several times Liepaja Theater has been nominated for Latvian Annual Amatheur theatre prize, which usually means to be in the Top 10 list of best plays during the year. “The long Christmas dinner” (Thornton Widler) played by Liepaja Theater was one of the top 10 plays in Latvia (the total number of amateur plays, which has been rated  is 70 plays) in 2014. Since 2008 Liepaja Theater hosts the international amateur theater festival ”Te un nekur citur” (”Here and nowhere else”) in Liepaja.

Leikfélag Selfoss was established in 1958 and has over the years put on various plays, both Icelandic and foreign, plays in full length and short stories, new and old.

The Three Ringings

The Three Ringings - by Kaj Nissen


We are transported back to the 16th century. Before the days of the reformation, around the year 1525. The Catholic Church and her priests have great importance, but many are still in awe of supernatural phenomena.

The piece describes a day laborers fight against the permanent harassment by his superior, the parish priest. One day, the lowly laborer Søren Bie has had enough and in a fit of rage, he kills the priest with his scythe.For his crime, Søren must be hanged, but the judge has an idea and converts Søren’s sentence: If Søren can ring the bells on three different churches in the county within two hours, he can escape the gallows. It’s going to be the struggle of his life, since Søren is not beloved by everyone. But many of the area’s children are without prejudice and fail to see a reason for Søren to be hanged. So they derive a cunning plan in order to help Søren out of his severe predicament. Will they succeed in helping him?

The first performance of Fyrspillene i Sydthy took place in 1984 on the 100 years’ anniversary celebrations of Lodbjerg Fyr (Lodbjerg lighthouse).

The idea of the plays is to have a performance at a new location in the municipality of Sydthy each year and having a new play written on basis of a story taking place in that very location. Fyrspillene have followed that concept for 27 years now, which is quite unique within Danish amateur theatre. Mostly, there are up to a 100 participants in each play. They are all volunteers - the only person receiving a salary is the playwright.

In 2003 the premises of the old elementary school in Gettrup in the southern part of Sydthy were placed at the disposal of Fyrspillene by the municipality of Sydthy. The comprehensive theatre wardrobe is now located there as well as a costume workroom and small props. In 2006 a large storage room was built next to the schoolhouse for big props, stands etc.

Trogolytes

Trogolytes - by Henning Jensen

 

Two men regain consciousness in a hole in the ground, which reminds vaguely of an enclosure for dangerous animals at a zoo. They have no idea of how they got there, or even of who they are or used to be. Slowly, memories return, but the colour of these memories remain open for vigorous discussion.  

Will their prayers for rescue be answered before nightfall, when the zoo closes down?

Are they actually alone in their prison? Who are they? What will they do? The answers to all questions will not be revealed in the play “Troglodytes”.

Actors: David Pind Jessen, Sven Rommenhøller, Direction:  Jacob B. Jessen, Translation:  Sven Rommenhøller. Duration: 50 minutes.

The ensemble was brought to life by teachers and students of Thisted Gymnasium in 1981. At first, it resided in several locations in Thisted, but in 1998 it returned to the house that spawned the company in the first place.  

The company has always consisted of students and teachers from Thisted Gymnasium, who by participating in “Amard”, also became members of DATS (Danish Amateur Theatre Association).

There have been numerous productions in the course of 34 years, and the range of plays has spanned wide. At the last festival in Thisted, the company presented a production of polish S. Mrozek’s “Out at Sea”. This year, another absurd play in one act was chosen: Henning Jensen’s: "Grottedyr" (Troglodytes).

 Founder and chieftain of Amard is Jacob B. Jessen.

Winners

"Winners" - by Brian Friel

 

Splódar Theatre Company presents this Brian Friel masterpiece which is sometimes referred to as the Romeo and Juliet of Irish theatre.

 

Mag, a pregnant teenager and Joe her boyfriend and husband to be spend the last day of their lives studying for their final examination on a hilltop overlooking their native town. There, by turns, they laugh, joke, quarrel and show a touching love for each other as they reminisce on the past and contemplate an uncertain future.

 

This marvellous evocation of young love by Ireland’s leading contemporary playwright stars two students of drama at Sligo Institute of Technology.


Splódar was founded in 1995. It is the resident company in the Glens Centre Theatre, Manorhamilton, Co. Leitrim, Ireland.

 

Splódar is perhaps unique in Ireland in that it presents plays in both the Irish and English languages. The works of well-known Irish writers such as James Joyce, Brian Friel, Frank McGuinness, John B. Keane and Tom Murphy have been staged over the years as well as dramas  by the internationally known writers, Noel Coward, Thornton Wilder, Garrison Keillor and Jean Anouilh.

 

On its previous visit to Thisted in 2006, Splódar presented the critically acclaimed At the Black Pig’s Dyke by Vincent Woods.
